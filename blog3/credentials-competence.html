Credentials vs Competence: Refuting Adele Tomlin’s Self-Proclaimed Authority

On the surface, these titles serve to create a halo of legitimacy, especially in Dharma spaces where Western academic credentials and legal knowledge carry unusual symbolic power. But when examined against her public behavior, errors in reasoning, and rhetorical patterns, the contradiction becomes too obvious to ignore:

Her behavior systematically violates the standards associated with the very professions she claims.

Claim #1: “I Was a Barrister”

Adele Tomlin claims she trained and worked as a barrister in the UK, a position that implies:

  • Fluency in reading legal texts, contracts, and court documents
  • Logical argumentation grounded in procedural clarity
  • Ethical standards around citation and representation of facts

But in her writings on the Karmapa court cases, she:

  • Treats civil filings as convictions
    (e.g., framing motions or complaints as if they are judicial conclusions)
  • Quotes selectively from judgments while ignoring exculpatory or neutral content
  • Uses loaded moral language without legal basis
  • Fails to distinguish between allegation, finding, and procedural delay

In several instances, she mistakes procedural events for moral proof, such as:

  • Assuming that a group filing a petition “proves” their guilt or ill motive
  • Misunderstanding lis pendens (pending litigation) as an automatic indictment
  • Ignoring burden of proof standards, as if mere accusation equals factual wrongdoing

A barrister would never do this.

  • Rigorous discipline of precision, especially when interpreting sacred or formal texts
