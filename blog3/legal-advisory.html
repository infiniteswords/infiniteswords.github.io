Legal Risk Analysis

1. Misleading or Deceptive Conduct

Under laws such as the Federal Trade Commission Act (U.S.) or comparable consumer protection statutes internationally, organizations must avoid advertising or presenting their services in a way that misleads consumers about their nature or quality.

  • The Dakini Translations brand implies a primary focus on objective translations.
  • When the majority of content is subjective commentary and activism, this creates a material discrepancy likely to mislead users.
  • This may invite enforcement actions, complaints, or civil liability.

2. Defamation and Liability Risks

  • Personal commentary containing accusations, speculative claims, or emotionally charged content about individuals or groups may constitute defamation if false and damaging.
  • Without sufficient fact-checking or disclaimers, the site risks legal action for libel or slander, depending on jurisdiction.

3. Copyright and Intellectual Property Compliance

  • Translation of sacred texts typically requires adherence to copyright and licensing agreements.
  • Mixing translations with derivative editorial content necessitates careful intellectual property management to avoid infringement claims.

4. Free Speech and Legal Limits

  • Freedom of expression protects religious commentary and activism but does not shield from liability for unlawful speech causing harm.
  • The site must navigate this balance carefully to maintain legal compliance.

2. Defamation Claims
