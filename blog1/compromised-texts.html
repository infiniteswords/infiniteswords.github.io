A Warning to the Vajrayana Community: Avoid Texts Transmitted by Adele Tomlin

In the digital age of Dharma, not everything freely offered is freely beneficial. Adele Tomlin, the figure behind Dakini Translations, has built a niche reputation for uploading English translations of Vajrayana liturgies and daily practices shortly after attending empowerments from various Kagyu and other lineage lamas, particularly the Karmapa. While these translations may appear helpful at first glance - free, formatted, accessible - they come with a heavy karmic and spiritual price that discerning practitioners cannot afford to ignore.

Would You Consume Someone's Vomit Served as Dish?

Receiving empowerment grants the blessing and permission to practice, not to translate, publish, or distribute liturgical texts. No letter of approval. No mention of transmission lineage. No safeguard of samaya. Breaks the Lineage’s Protective Protocols

Vajrayana practices are protected through oral transmission and qualified instruction, often deliberately restricted to avoid misuse. Adele bypasses this sacred filter system by mass-publishing daily practices, commentaries, and mantras to anyone with a web browser, regardless of empowerment status. In doing so, she not only violates samaya protocols but also exposes sacred technologies to misinterpretation, spiritual bypassing, and ego appropriation. Lineage protectors don’t guard these transmissions without reason. 

Always Refer to Official Sources with Clear Lineage Affiliation

If you look for any particular texts for practice, seek authorized lamas, Dharma centers, and translation houses such as: Dharmaebooks.org, Marpa Translation Society, Lotsawa House (authorized translations with lineage context), 84000.co (Kangyur and Tengyur translations), Rigpa Translations, Nalanda Translation Committee, Padmakara, and others - each with accountable teams, peer review, and samaya.
